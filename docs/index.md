## Report Generation {#report-generation}

When I first joined this company, it was because I wanted to be helpful to the entire process of constructing a neuromodulation device and leverage my unique expertise in psychology, neuroimaging and research to be an effective developer of this device's features.

What we figured was the most immediate fit was the fact that the EEG part of the trifecta (tDCS+CET+EEG) is most underdeveloped, and having worked in an EEG lab that was the most critical question, and as Saakshi asked me in the very first meeting; given the raw EEG data we obtain, how best to get something useful out of it?

Through a series of events, we generated many different reports, using both my manual editing and coding repos, from the first hand-typed report containing hemispherical symmetry information and relative PSD value graphs generated by the code (as presented by Dr Bannerjee in ANCIPS), to the final version live right now, which uses two scripts, `core_processing_pipeline.py` and `json_export.py`, with the latter calling functions from the former to generate a json file with pre-post comparison metrics, that can be converted by Ashish's code to fill out the pdf we generate on doctor dashboard, while `core_processing_pipeline.py` is capable of generating detailed metrics for a single EEG session.

Then, we made an HTML document to infer it in detail, usable for sales, patients and doctors;

Finally set up a repo to use json notation, css styling and react for rendering and saving pdfs, that can now generate versatile documents with appropriate sources for design and content!

## Patents {#patents}

A detailed analysis of Indian, international and foreign patent laws helped us define key concepts, of which 'Freedom To Operate' became a central theme: we understood broad claims of methods and systems (such as those by Flow, Soterix) to be formed to restrict others from making and selling similar devices, while narrower claims of devices and applications (such as those listed by Swapnil, Boston Engineering) to strengthen one's own niche and prevent prosecution from broader patents as defined before. Further clarity on how Freedom to Operate works out practically was demonstrated with the case study on Oura, with a special emphasis of their prosecution of Ultrahuman, which taught us the importance of both a) designing around existing prior art and b) how a patent can be used to prosecute similar products.

A two-pronged approach to writing patents was identified, a top-down approach aimed at writing a broad patent that claims a novel system of neuromodulation, and specific continuation and/or parallel patents which change one aspect of our system (monitoring, interactive display/therapeutic techniques, stimulation) and write a continuation patent. Close patents are analyzed for the scope and the limitations of their claims, as well as IPC indices for the same.

The substantive, novel step synthesized from this analysis was that the prior art of a closed-loop system of neuromodulation has been patented, however, the technique to personalize it, using data that is outside the system is not considered. Instead of detect state-> trigger modulation, we specify logic of decision making (control policy) that considers patient characteristics, subjective information and other inputs to decide therapy as a whole, not just stimulation.

## Signal Quality {#signal-quality}

In an effort to test the relative signal quality from 220k ohm resistor EEG compared to the 47k ohm one, Swapnil Lakshay and I concluded the best way to experimentally validate digital signaling decisions would be to first see how the functions work on known, ideal data recorded with our EEG electrodes.

This gave a new opportunity to rebuild our pipeline from scratch in a novel way, i.e. calibrating the dsp modules to known hardware properties in a sequence of steps that additively boost signal-to-noise ratio and reliability.

The first completed part of this exists within the signal quality repo, which has modules for designing different filters (types and parameters), FFT calculations, visualization, calibration and testing scripts. Optimization scripts can test different combinations of filter types, parameters and sequences of steps, and compare results to known test data (included under hardware lab) to select the modules which work best for us. After running experimental and test scripts, final decisions were compiled in detect_sine.py which has been verified to correctly identify the frequency/amplitude of generated ideal waves and reconstruct them in the time domain.

This will be followed by ERP experimentations (where averaged trials show known peaks) and artefact generation protocols to continue refining our dsp modules and reach state-of-the-art reliability, which this documentation will help to construct into a narrative for publishable results in a scientific journal.
